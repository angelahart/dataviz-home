Big Project

Pitch: It is estimated that 2.4 million Californians will gain health insurance coverage under the Affordable Care Act, through expanded Medi-Cal, or the state health insurance marketplace. But health experts say the population who will remain ineligible for health insurance even after Jan. 1 is nearly double that — up to 4 million people. Three-quarters of those left out of health reform will not be able to get covered because of their immigration status.

News Angle

The Affordable Care Act goes into effect Jan. 1. Meanwhile, the president is pushing what arguably is his second biggest policy agenda — immigration reform. This map will look at the intersection of the two in California
